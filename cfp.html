Submission Instructions

Please submit an extended abstract or position paper of 2 to 4 pages in IEEE format (excluding references) via the workshop's submission system (link coming soon). Submissions may also include supplementary video material, demos, or visual content, especially when illustrating geometric structure, contact-rich interaction, or representation-driven manipulation behaviour.

All submissions will undergo peer review by at least two domain experts, with attention to:

  • Relevance to the workshop themes
  • Technical quality and novelty
  • Potential to stimulate discussion during the workshop

Accepted contributions will be presented through spotlight teaser talks and interactive poster sessions, giving contributors visibility and direct access to feedback from invited speakers and attendees. Outstanding accepted authors may also be invited to participate in the Interdisciplinary Impact Clinics (IIC) for live mentoring from senior experts.

Topics of Interest

We welcome contributions on (but not limited to) the following topics:

  • Geometric priors and groundings for sensing and state estimation on manifolds (SE(3), SO(3), etc.)
  • Equivariant and symmetry-aware models for robot perception and manipulation
  • Structure-preserving learning and manifold-valued motor skills
  • Learning on Riemannian manifolds and Lie groups
  • Geometry-aware motion planning and trajectory optimization
  • Geometric mechanics and contact-rich manipulation
  • Task-space control with geometric metrics and constraints
  • Geometric world models, affordances, and generative representations
  • Object-centric and scene-centric representations for manipulation
  • Geometric foundations for whole-body and dexterous manipulation
  • Composability and generalization through geometric structure
  • Applications: dexterous manipulation, bimanual tasks, human-robot interaction

We especially encourage submissions from women, early-career researchers, and interdisciplinary teams. Submissions that connect traditionally separate communities (e.g., geometric control + robot learning, tactile sensing + manifold methods) are particularly welcome.
